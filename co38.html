11+ Comprehension (CO38)

Time allowed: 30 minutes

Passage

Our school’s lost-property cupboard was a mountain of odd gloves and nameless jumpers. Parents complained; pupils avoided the smell. The digital club proposed a simple overhaul: a weekly photo board, a coding system by colour and size, and a rule that anything unclaimed after a term would be washed and donated. We built a light box from a cardboard frame and white cloth so photos were clear. Each item got a code: category letter (J for jumper, H for hat), a colour tag, and size. We posted a grid of thumbnails on the notice board and online every Friday with pick-up hours. A Year 6 team ran “match hour,” helping younger pupils search by code—J/Blue/Small—before home time. The first week revealed surprises. Items with clear names stitched in were collected immediately; anonymous water bottles lingered. We added a pen station labelled “Write your name—save a trip” and a box of iron-on labels for a small donation. We also set a “pairing peg” for single gloves; when a match arrived, a monitor clipped them together like a reunion picture. By the end of term, the cupboard had space to breathe. A washing rota kept smells away, and donations went to a local charity shop with a signed receipt. The head of year said parents had stopped emailing “Has anyone seen my child’s jumper?” and started writing names instead. Our board didn’t magic things back to owners; it made finding fair and fast.
