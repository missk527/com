11+ Comprehension (CO35)

Time allowed: 30 minutes

Passage

Our town’s park hosted a monthly evening run. As autumn darkened, some turns felt unsafe. We planned a low-glare lighting trial along the 3 km loop using rechargeable lanterns on short posts and reflective arrows at ankle height. The rules: lights off by 9 p.m., no lights near the bat roost, and set lanterns to warm white to protect night vision. We measured a test kilometre and placed markers at bends, roots, and narrow bridges. Runners wore small clip-on reflectors. On trial night, a crew cycled ahead to switch lanterns on and check for fallen branches. The course felt calmer, and finish times tightened as fewer runners slowed to peer into shadows. A volunteer with a decibel meter showed that lanterns didn’t increase crowd noise; the loudest moments were still at the start and finish. Two issues arose. Fog rolled in and made distant lights halo; we lowered post height and added extra reflectors instead of more lumens. A neighbour worried about light spill near her window; we fitted simple caps that shaded the back half of the lanterns. We also mapped where bats crossed an open stretch and shifted one arrow to avoid shining into their flight line. At the debrief, the club voted for a mixed kit: lanterns for corners and bridges, reflectors for straight paths, and chalk marks for temporary mud. We posted a guide: charge lanterns in the morning, check caps, and collect all gear by 9:15. The park returned to darkness, but the memory of the route remained lit in people’s legs.
