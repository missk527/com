Time allowed: 30 minutes

Passage

On the last Saturday of autumn, eleven-year-old Marcus and his cousin Leah were sent to help clean out their grandmother’s attic. At first, they complained about the dust and the strange smell of old wood and mothballs. However, as they pushed aside faded suitcases and yellowed curtains, Leah spotted a wooden box with a metal latch hidden behind a stack of picture frames. The box had swirling carvings along the edges and a tiny keyhole shaped like a leaf. They searched the attic for a key but found nothing. Marcus tried to pry it open with a hairpin he found in a jewelry tin, but it only rattled. When their grandmother came upstairs with hot chocolate, she froze for a moment upon seeing the box. Then she smiled and said it had belonged to her father, who had traveled across the sea as a young man. She didn’t know where the key had gone, but she encouraged them to keep looking. Later that afternoon, Leah found an old coat with deep inside pockets. In one of them, wrapped in tissue paper, lay a small brass key, cold and heavy in her hand. The children rushed back to the box and carefully fit the key in the lock. It clicked open with a soft snap. Inside, they found a leather-bound journal filled with sketches of ships, islands, and unfamiliar plants. There were also letters in neat, slanted handwriting addressed to someone named “Elena,” and a silver compass with a cracked glass face. Marcus and Leah sat on the attic floor reading the first few pages of the journal. It described storms on the ocean, trading goods in distant ports, and once rescuing a stranded sailor. Some pages were written in another language neither of them recognized. When their grandmother returned and saw the journal open, her eyes grew misty. She explained that Elena was her mother and that the journal recorded her parents’ early adventures together before they settled in their hometown. Instead of putting the box away, their grandmother asked the children to help her store the contents carefully in a new chest downstairs, where everyone could see them. Marcus promised to research the unknown language, while Leah planned to draw a map of the places mentioned in the journal. They no longer saw the attic as dull or dusty; it had become the beginning of a mystery they were eager to explore.
