11+ Comprehension (CO39)

Time allowed: 30 minutes

Passage

Our local museum had more stories than space. We borrowed a minibus and turned it into a travelling recording studio—the History Bus. Seats faced a small table; a soft lamp warmed the corner; a microphone hung on a swing arm. We parked outside the market and asked elders to share memories of how the town changed—when washing still went through a mangle, when the theatre ran double bills, when the river froze. We set rules: recordings lasted no more than ten minutes; each guest approved their clip on headphones; and no politics or personal disputes. A drama student greeted visitors and offered tea; a tech student checked levels and pressed record. We wrote consent forms in large print and added a checkbox for “museum use only” or “school projects allowed.” By noon the hard drive filled with seconds of treasure—laughter over a bicycle with no tyres, a description of a victory parade, a recipe for wartime jam without sugar. One storyteller brought a shoebox of photos; we scanned them on the table and matched captions to street corners we could still recognise. A child asked whether people were happier then. A baker answered gently, “People were busy then, and we are busy now; stories help us notice what stays.” The bus visited the care home on Friday. There, memories came in careful pieces. We kept sessions shorter and returned to the same residents the next week to add layers. Our poster at school displayed a QR code to a curated playlist and a map of where each story was recorded. The minibus went back on Monday, but the voices kept visiting us through small speakers that carried time.
